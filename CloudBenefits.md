| Term                  | Meaning                                           |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| **Agility**           | Quickly provision and deploy resources            |
| **Elasticity**        | Automatically scale resources up/down with demand |
| **Scalability**       | Increase capacity to handle growth                |
| **High Availability** | Remain operational during failures                |
| **Fault Tolerance**   | Continue operating despite component failures     |
| **Reliability**       | Consistently perform as expected                  |
| **Global Reach**      | Deploy around the world easily                    |
| **Cost Optimization** | Pay only for what you use                         |

1. **Trade capital expense (CapEx) for variable expense (OpEx)**

   * Pay only for the resources you use instead of investing heavily in data centers and hardware upfront.

2. **Benefit from massive economies of scale**

   * AWS's large scale leads to lower variable costs than most organizations can achieve on their own.

3. **Stop guessing capacity**

   * Scale resources up or down based on demand instead of overprovisioning or underprovisioning.

4. **Increase speed and agility**

   * Provision resources in minutes rather than waiting weeks or months for hardware procurement.

5. **Stop spending money running and maintaining data centers**

   * AWS handles the infrastructure, allowing you to focus on your applications and business.

6. **Go global in minutes**

   * Deploy applications in multiple AWS Regions around the world quickly and easily.

### Quick memory trick

**C-E-C-S-D-G**

* **C**apEx → OpEx
* **E**conomies of scale
* **C**apacity guessing (stop)
* **S**peed and agility
* **D**ata center maintenance (stop spending on it)
* **G**o global
